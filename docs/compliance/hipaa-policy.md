## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for protecting Protected Health Information (PHI) in compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) of 1996.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees of Hello Gorgeous Med Spa
- All contractors and vendors with access to PHI
- All electronic systems containing PHI
- All paper records containing PHI

---

## 3. Definitions

**Protected Health Information (PHI):** Any individually identifiable health information including:
- Patient names
- Addresses (including zip codes)
- Dates (birth, treatment, death)
- Phone numbers
- Email addresses
- Social Security numbers
- Medical record numbers
- Health plan numbers
- Account numbers
- Photos
- Any unique identifying characteristic

**Electronic PHI (ePHI):** PHI stored, transmitted, or received electronically.

**Covered Entity:** Hello Gorgeous Med Spa, as a healthcare provider.

**Business Associate:** Third-party vendors who handle PHI on our behalf.

---

## 4. Privacy Standards

### 4.1 Minimum Necessary Standard
Access to PHI is limited to the minimum necessary to accomplish the intended purpose.

### 4.2 Use and Disclosure
PHI may only be used or disclosed for:
- Treatment purposes
- Payment operations
- Healthcare operations
- With patient authorization
- As required by law

### 4.3 Patient Rights
Patients have the right to:
- Access their health records
- Request amendments to records
- Receive accounting of disclosures
- Request restrictions on use/disclosure
- Request confidential communications
- File complaints

## 5. Security Standards

### 5.1 Administrative Safeguards

| Safeguard | Implementation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Security Officer | Owner/designated manager |
| Workforce Training | Annual HIPAA training required |
| Access Management | Role-based access controls |
| Incident Response | Documented response plan |
| Business Associates | BAAs with all vendors |

### 5.2 Physical Safeguards

| Safeguard | Implementation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Facility Access | Locked doors, security system |
| Workstation Security | Screen locks, privacy screens |
| Device Controls | Secured storage, disposal procedures |
| Paper Records | Locked cabinets, shred policy |

### 5.3 Technical Safeguards

| Safeguard | Implementation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Access Control | Unique user IDs, passwords |
| Audit Controls | System logging enabled |
| Integrity | Data validation, backups |
| Transmission Security | 256-bit SSL encryption |
| Encryption | Data encrypted at rest and in transit |

## 6. Electronic Systems

### 6.1 Hello Gorgeous OS (Supabase)

## 7. Workforce Requirements

### 7.1 Training

- Initial HIPAA training (within 30 days of hire)

## 9. Breach Notification

### 9.1 Definition
A breach is unauthorized access, use, or disclosure of PHI that compromises security or privacy.

### 9.2 Response Timeline

| Action | Timeline |
|--------|----------|
| Contain breach | Immediately |
| Investigate | Within 24 hours |
| Risk assessment | Within 48 hours |
| Notify individuals | Within 60 days |
| Notify HHS | Within 60 days |
| Notify media (500+) | Within 60 days |

### 9.3 Documentation
All breaches must be documented including:
- Date discovered
- Type of PHI involved
- Number of individuals affected
- Actions taken
- Prevention measures implemented

---

## 10. Sanctions

### 10.1 Workforce Violations

| Violation Level | Consequence |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Unintentional | Retraining required |
| Negligent | Written warning |
| Repeated | Suspension |
| Willful | Termination |

### 10.2 Federal Penalties
HIPAA violations can result in:
- Civil fines: $100 - $50,000 per violation
- Criminal penalties: Up to $250,000 and imprisonment
- State attorney general actions
