## Illinois Med Spa Regulations

### Supervising Physician Requirements

In Illinois, med spas performing medical procedures must have:

1. **Supervising Physician**
   - Licensed MD or DO in Illinois
   - Active, unrestricted license
   - Collaborative agreement with mid-levels
   - Available for consultation

2. **Written Protocols**
   - Standing orders for procedures
   - Emergency protocols
   - Drug administration guidelines

3. **On-Site vs Remote Supervision**
   - Higher-risk procedures: On-site preferred
   - Lower-risk: Remote supervision acceptable
   - Must be reachable by phone

## Provider Credentials

### Advanced Practice Registered Nurse (APRN)

**Required:**
- Active Illinois APRN license
- National certification (FNP-BC, AGPCNP-BC, etc.)
- Collaborative agreement with supervising physician
- DEA license (if prescribing)
- NPI number

**Scope of Practice:**
- Perform aesthetic procedures
- Prescribe medications
- Order labs
- Document in medical record
- Supervise medical assistants

**Renewal:**
- APRN license: Every 2 years
- National certification: Per certifying body
- DEA: Every 3 years
- CPR/BLS: Every 2 years

---

### Physician Assistant (PA)

**Required:**
- Active Illinois PA license
- National certification (NCCPA)
- Supervision agreement with physician
- DEA license (if prescribing)
- NPI number

**Scope of Practice:**
- Similar to APRN under supervision
- Dependent on supervising physician's specialty

**Renewal:**
- PA license: Every 2 years
- NCCPA: Every 2 years (with CME)
- DEA: Every 3 years

---

### Registered Nurse (RN)

**Required:**
- Active Illinois RN license
- BLS certification
- Specific procedure training

**Scope of Practice (Under Supervision):**
- Administer injections (Botox, fillers)
- IV therapy
- Assist with procedures
- Patient education

**Cannot:**
- Prescribe medications
- Practice independently
- Perform procedures without orders

**Renewal:**
- RN license: Every 2 years
- BLS: Every 2 years

---

### Licensed Esthetician

**Required:**
- Illinois Esthetician license
- Continuing education credits

**Scope of Practice:**
- Facials
- Dermaplaning
- Chemical peels (superficial)
- Microdermabrasion
- Lash services
- Brow services

**Cannot:**
- Perform injections
- Laser treatments (without additional certification)
- Medical-grade peels
- Any invasive procedures

**Renewal:**
- License: Every 2 years
- CE: 14 hours per renewal

---

### Medical Assistant

**Required:**
- Certification preferred (CMA, RMA)
- On-the-job training acceptable
- CPR/BLS certification

**Scope of Practice:**
- Assist with procedures
- Take vital signs
- Patient intake
- Room preparation
- Administrative tasks

**Cannot:**
- Perform procedures independently
- Administer medications without delegation
- Make clinical decisions

## Collaborative Practice Agreement

### Illinois Requirements

APRNs must have written collaborative agreement including:

1. **Identification**
   - APRN name and credentials
   - Collaborating physician(s)
   - Practice location(s)

2. **Scope Description**
   - Procedures authorized
   - Medications prescribable
   - Limitations

3. **Consultation Requirements**
   - When to consult
   - Communication methods
   - Availability requirements

4. **Prescriptive Authority**
   - Schedule II-V (if applicable)
   - Drug categories
   - Limitations

5. **Signatures**
   - Both parties
   - Dated
   - Reviewed annually

## Malpractice Insurance

### Requirements

All providers must maintain:
- Minimum $1M per occurrence
- Minimum $3M aggregate
- Coverage for specific procedures performed
- Tail coverage if leaving

### Documentation

Keep on file:
- Certificate of insurance
- Policy effective dates
- Coverage limits
- Named insured
- Covered procedures

### Verification

- Verify annually
- 30-day notice if cancelled
- Update immediately if changed

## DEA Registration

### Requirements

For providers prescribing controlled substances:
- DEA registration at practice location
- State controlled substance license (if required)
- Linked to collaborating physician (if applicable)

### Compliance

- Secure storage of medications
- Proper documentation
- Disposal protocols
- Inventory tracking

## Competency Assessment

### New Procedures

Before provider performs new procedure:
1. Complete training
2. Observe experienced provider
3. Perform under supervision (3-5 cases)
4. Competency sign-off
5. Independent practice

### Annual Assessment

All providers assessed annually on:
- Technique
- Complication management
- Emergency response
- Documentation quality
- Patient communication
