## Medical Emergencies

### Anaphylaxis Protocol

**Signs/Symptoms:**
- Difficulty breathing
- Swelling of face/throat
- Rapid heartbeat
- Hives/rash spreading
- Drop in blood pressure
- Confusion/anxiety

**Immediate Actions:**

```
1. CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY
2. Position patient flat (legs elevated if not breathing difficulty)
3. Administer Epinephrine (EpiPen):
   - Adults: 0.3mg IM in outer thigh
   - Remove safety cap
   - Press firmly until click
   - Hold for 10 seconds
4. Start timing (note time of EpiPen)
5. Monitor airway and breathing
6. Prepare for CPR if needed
7. Second EpiPen after 5-15 min if no improvement
8. Stay with patient until EMS arrives
```

**Emergency Kit Contents:**
- EpiPen (2) - Check expiration monthly
- Benadryl (diphenhydramine)
- Blood pressure cuff
- Pulse oximeter
- Oxygen (if available)
- Emergency contact list

### Vascular Occlusion Protocol

**Signs/Symptoms:**
- Blanching (white area after injection)
- Severe pain disproportionate to procedure
- Dusky/blue discoloration
- Mottled skin
- Vision changes (if near eye)

**Immediate Actions:**

```
1. STOP INJECTION IMMEDIATELY
2. Do NOT massage area
3. Apply warm compress
4. Inject Hyaluronidase:
   - 150-300 units per affected area
   - Inject into and around affected tissue
   - May repeat every 60-90 minutes
5. Apply nitroglycerin paste 2% (if available)
6. Contact supervising physician IMMEDIATELY
7. Document with photos every 15 minutes
8. Consider emergency transfer if:
   - Vision affected
   - No improvement after hyaluronidase
   - Tissue necrosis developing
```

### Syncope (Fainting) Protocol

**Signs/Symptoms:**
- Pale, sweaty skin
- Lightheadedness
- Nausea
- Loss of consciousness

**Immediate Actions:**

```
1. Ease patient to floor/recline position
2. Elevate legs above heart level
3. Loosen tight clothing
4. Ensure open airway
5. Apply cool compress to forehead
6. Monitor breathing and pulse
7. Do NOT give food/water until fully alert
8. Allow slow recovery (10-15 minutes)
9. Document incident
```

**When to Call 911:**
- Unconscious more than 1 minute
- Irregular heartbeat
- Chest pain
- Difficulty breathing
- Injury from fall

---

### Seizure Protocol

**Immediate Actions:**

```
1. CALL 911
2. Clear area of hazards
3. Do NOT restrain patient
4. Do NOT put anything in mouth
5. Time the seizure
6. Position on side after seizure stops
7. Stay with patient
8. Do NOT give food/water
9. Document details
```

## Adverse Reactions

### Minor Adverse Events

**Examples:**
- Bruising (expected)
- Mild swelling
- Redness at injection site
- Headache
- Minor asymmetry

**Response:**
1. Reassure patient (normal and expected)
2. Provide aftercare instructions
3. Offer follow-up appointment
4. Document in chart
5. No incident report required

---

### Moderate Adverse Events

**Examples:**
- Significant bruising
- Prolonged swelling
- Mild infection
- Unexpected asymmetry
- Lumps/nodules

**Response:**
1. Assess severity
2. Provide treatment/intervention
3. Schedule follow-up within 48 hours
4. Document thoroughly with photos
5. Complete incident report
6. Notify supervising physician

---

### Severe Adverse Events

**Examples:**
- Vascular occlusion
- Severe allergic reaction
- Skin necrosis
- Vision changes
- Nerve damage
- Significant infection

**Response:**
1. Follow emergency protocol
2. Call 911 if life-threatening
3. Contact supervising physician immediately
4. Document everything with photos
5. Complete detailed incident report
6. Notify malpractice insurance
7. Preserve all product information (lot numbers)
8. Do not discard any materials

## Data Breach Response

### Definition
Unauthorized access, acquisition, use, or disclosure of PHI that compromises security or privacy.

### Breach Discovery

```
1. IMMEDIATELY notify Privacy Officer
2. Do NOT continue accessing affected system
3. Document what you observed:
   - Date and time discovered
   - What was accessed
   - How it was discovered
   - Who was involved
```

### Investigation (Within 24 Hours)

```
1. Privacy Officer assembles response team
2. Contain the breach:
   - Disable compromised accounts
   - Secure affected systems
   - Preserve evidence
3. Determine scope:
   - What PHI was involved?
   - How many individuals affected?
   - Was data encrypted?
4. Document findings
```

### Risk Assessment (Within 48 Hours)

Evaluate whether notification required based on:
- Nature of PHI involved
- Unauthorized person who accessed
- Whether PHI was actually acquired/viewed
- Extent to which risk has been mitigated

### Notification Requirements

| Notification | Timeline | Trigger |
|--------------|----------|---------|
| Affected Individuals | 60 days | Breach confirmed |
| HHS | 60 days | Breach confirmed |
| Media | 60 days | 500+ individuals in state |

### Notification Content

Must include:
- Description of breach
- Types of information involved
- Steps to protect themselves
- What we're doing in response
- Contact information for questions

## Client Complaints

### Receiving Complaints

```
1. Listen without interrupting
2. Express empathy
3. Take detailed notes
4. Do NOT admit fault or liability
5. Explain next steps
6. Provide timeline for response
```

### Documentation

Record:
- Date and time of complaint
- Client name and contact
- Nature of complaint
- Services involved
- Staff involved
- Desired resolution
- Your response

### Investigation

```
1. Review treatment records
2. Review consent forms
3. Interview staff involved
4. Review photos (before/after)
5. Consult with supervising physician
6. Determine appropriate resolution
```

### Resolution Options

| Level | Resolution |
|-------|------------|
| Minor | Apology, follow-up care |
| Moderate | Complimentary correction, discount |
| Significant | Refund, additional treatment |
| Severe | Legal consultation, insurance notification |

### Escalation

Immediately escalate to owner/legal if:
- Threat of lawsuit
- Request for medical records
- Contact from attorney
- Mention of reporting to medical board
- Social media threats
